Facebook after death: 5 tips for managing your account in the afterlife

Gina Hall, Contributor

Everyone has one on their Facebook page — the deceased acquaintance or family member still on their friends list, sitting idle, as if about to post their latest status update.

Facebook Inc. finally added a much-needed "legacy contact" feature Thursday, allowing users to designate a friend or loved one to manage aspects of their account after they pass away.

"Until now, when someone passed away, we offered a basic memorialized account which was viewable, but could not be managed by anyone," Facbook wrote on its blog. "By talking to people who have experienced loss, we realized there is more we can do to support those who are grieving and those who want a say in what happens to their account after death."

Like writing a will, it's not pleasant to think about how to manage your social media presence when you're gone, but it has become a necessity. Here are a few things you should know about how to handle your Facebook settings when you join the great social platform in the sky.

1. Pick a legacy contact.
You can now pick a trusted family member or friend to manage your page after you die. Facebook is following in the steps of Google, which began to allow users to select digital heirs to manage "inactive accounts" back in 2013.

Facebook legacy contacts can pin a post at the top of your memorialized profile page, change your profile picture and even respond to new friend requests on your behalf. To pick a legacy contact, visit the security settings on your Facebook page.

2. Think about how you'd like to be remembered
 Do you want people to read your obituary? With the death of print, the newspaper may not be the place to list your life's accomplishments. Realize that Facebook may be where most of your friends and family may come to learn about funeral arrangements or honor your memory.
