Tech sector can weather currency headwinds

By Caitlin Huston

Published: Feb 18, 2015 11:27 a.m. ET

Share

By

CaitlinHuston

Reporter

NEW YORK (MarketWatch)—Though the tech sector may be the most affected by currency headwinds, profits will grow in 2015, according to Deutsche Bank analysts.

The tech sector earns an estimated 37% of profits in foreign currency, making it vulnerable to the recent strengthening of the U.S. dollar, they wrote in a note. Nearly all semiconductor sales and a large part of foreign sales to corporate customers in software and other sectors are made in dollars. The tech sector is facing a roughly 4% to 5% headwind from the dollar, compared with a 2.5% headwind for the S&P 500, they wrote.

But Deutsche analysts are expecting per-share earnings growth of 4% to 7% for the tech sector in 2015, and say it has the highest upside potential of any sector in the S&P 500, especially if the dollar keeps long-term real interest rates low.

U.S. tech companies dominate their foreign rivals. They stand to benefit from the possibility of increased U.S. and European spending on tech equipment, software and services.

Tech companies have access to cheap borrowing rates, given that they are not highly leveraged and can tap the bond market at long-term rates of less than 3%. Deutsche believes there is a strong chance that a Republican-led Congress will implement a repatriation tax holiday.

“Repatriations should be large and stock up if the rate is cut to ~5% or less,” said the note.

The tech sector pay decent dividends and is demonstrating strong growth in dividend-per shares on higher net income and payout ratios, said the note. The sector’s current total yield is 4.0% and payout ratio is 71% adjusted for stock options. That compares with 3.6% and 63% for the broader S&P.

“We prefer tech over other globally exposed cyclical sectors and highlight a tradable basket of 12 big-cap reasonable PE,” said the note.

These include Apple Inc. AAPL, +0.69% Microsoft Corp. MSFT, -0.11%  and Google Inc. GOOG, -0.58%
