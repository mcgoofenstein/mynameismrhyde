    Why Facebook’s ad price rapidly increased in the fourth quarter

    By Patricia Garner 1 hour ago
    • 
    • 
    • 
    • 
    Content preferences

      Overview of Facebook's 4Q14 earnings and business developments (Part 3 of 10)

      (Continued from Part 2)

      Ad price growth

      During the fourth quarter, the average price per ad for Facebook (FB) grew at a year-over-year rate of 335%. By comparison, Twitter (TWTR) saw its advertising revenue per thousand timeline views increase by 60% year-over-year to $2.37 in 4Q14.

      Facebook’s average price per ad grew mainly because the company had redesigned its right-hand column ads. This offered more value to marketers and resulted in higher effective prices. Right-hand column ads are consistent with news feed ads, but the former are larger in size and fewer in number. For more on this topic, read Market Realist’s article Why new right-hand column design benefits Facebook.

      As the chart above shows, the growth of Facebook’s average price per ad was higher in the last quarter than it was in any other quarter of 2014.

      Better ad targeting

      Facebook is focusing on fewer ads to drive user engagement. This eventually leads to better return on investment for Facebook’s advertisers and results in better ad pricing. Facebook’s management said in an earnings call that the company is seeing a big opportunity here and will continue to drive engagement numbers by placing right-hand column ads in front of the right people.

      So far, the company believes that it has been successful in driving organic engagement growth. This is critical for any business.

      Declining ad impressions

      Right-hand column ads are not visible on mobile devices because less screen space is available. Also, mobile usage is increasing more quickly than desktop usage. As a result, right-hand column ads could become less valuable for Facebook as mobile penetration increases.

      Limited mobile device screen space is also the reason for the decline in ad impressions. Facebook has done well to challenge Google Inc. (GOOG)(GOOGL) so far in this space.

      Facebook’s average ad price increased at a rapid rate in 4Q14 mainly due to the following factors:

      • redesign of ads
      • improved ad targeting
      • launch of accurate measurement tools
      Investing in Facebook

      To gain portfolio exposure to Facebook, investors should consider the Technology Select Sector SPDR ETF (XLK). This ETF has a 4.1% Facebook weightage.
