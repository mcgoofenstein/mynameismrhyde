Dropbox replaces insider as CFO in move that may signal nearing IPO

Dropbox CEO Drew Houston has replaced an insider as CFO with a more seasoned public finance chief in a move that may signal he is getting closer to taking the company public.

Dropbox Inc. has brought a more experienced finance chief on board in a move seen as a sign that the file-sharing company is getting closer to finally going public.

The San Francisco company said that Vanessa Wittman, who was previously the finance chief at Google's Motorola unit, will become its chief financial officer next month.

Sujay Jaswa, a four-year veteran of Dropbox whose promotion to CFO was surprising last year because he didn't have experience in finance at a public company. He is reportedly leaving now for a career in investing.

Wittman is joining another former Motorola executive at Dropbox, Dennis Woodside, who was named chief operating officer last year.

In addition to her time in charge of finance at Motorola, Wittman has also been CFO of publicly-traded consultancy Marsh & McLennan and held top posts at Microsoft and Morgan Stanley.

"There are very few companies that have the potential to completely shape the way we interact with technology on a daily basis," Wittman said in a statement emailed to the media. "Dropbox, in a short period of time, has impacted the lives of more than 300 million people, who use it every day to share and safeguard their most important information."

Dropbox, led by CEO Drew Houston, is expected to follow its smaller storage and collaboration rival Box into the public markets this year. Box's stock is up about 50 percent above the price it went public at on Jan. 23, when it raised $175 million.

Dropbox has a much larger user base than Box since it went for the consumer storage market rather then the enterprise customers that its Los Altos-based rival has concentrated on. But it, too, has shifted focus to business customers as the margins on storage have dropped dramatically in the face of competition from tech giants like Google, Microsoft and Apple.
