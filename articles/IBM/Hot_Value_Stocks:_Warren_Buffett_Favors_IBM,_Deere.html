Hot Value Stocks: Warren Buffett Favors IBM, Deere

Thu, Feb 19 2015 00:00:00 E B06_DATA

BY KEN SHREVE, INVESTOR'S BUSINESS DAILY

05:47 PM ET
  • Email
  • Print
  • Reprints
  • Comment

Portfolio activity by Warren Buffett made headlines Wednesday. One move was consistent with his buy low, sell high strategy. The other move shows the famed investor might have an interest in the cup-with-handle pattern after all.

The legendary value investor increased his long-term bet in beaten-down IBM (NYSE:IBM), tracked in IBD's Computer-Tech Services group. He also announced a new position in John Deere (NYSE:DE), a member of the lagging Machinery-Farm group.

Buffett increased his stake in IBM by 6.5 million shares. He now owns close to 77 million shares, or a 7.8% stake, Bloomberg reported.

It's a bet that CEO Ginny Rometty will be able to turn the troubled company around, focusing more on cloud computing and data analytics. It's a booming field, but one that IBM was slow to embrace. Its stock price has suffered as a result.

Headed into Wednesday, six names in the tech services group had Composite Ratings of 96 or higher. Among them, Cognizant Technology (NASDAQ:CTSH) hasn't looked back after clearing a long consolidation in November. Late last week, Epam Systems (NYSE:EPAM) cleared a cup-shaped base with a buy point of 52.99. Tyler Technologies (NYSE:TYL), meanwhile, is flirting with a flat-base breakout over 115.47.

Unlike IBM, all three leaders are seeing strong demand for their services, and that's fueling price performance.

Meanwhile, Deere gapped out of a cup-with-handle base Wednesday with a buy point of 91.48, fueled by news of the Buffett stake.

Earnings and sales growth has declined for three straight quarters, though, and future growth prospects don't look much better. Fiscal 2015 earnings are seen falling 36% in fiscal 2015 (ending in October) and another 2% in fiscal 2016.
