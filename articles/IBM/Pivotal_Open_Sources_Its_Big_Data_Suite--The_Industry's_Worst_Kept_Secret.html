    Pivotal Open Sources Its Big Data Suite--The Industry's Worst Kept Secret

    Comment Now
    Follow Comments Following Comments Unfollow Comments

    For the past few months there has been a growing suggestion that Pivotal, the spin out company created with assets from both VMware and EMC, was about to announce something big in relation to its big data offering. Pivotal’s Hadoop distribution, a big data capture, storage, processing and analysis solution, has had far less uptake than better known solutions from Cloudera, MapR and Hortonworks. Late last year the allegation was made that Pivotal had laid off about 60 employees, most of them focused on big data. Pivotal chose not to comment on the layoffs, but CEO Paul Maritz did make some comments recently around a change in the company’s approach towards its big data offering. In reflecting upon the rumor that Pivotal was to open source its Hadoop distro, big data analyst Paul Miller quipped that “one less distro would not be a bad thing!”

    In the run up to a series of announcements from Pivotal this morning, I spent time with Sundeep Madra (VP of Data Products) and Mike Cucchi (Senior Director of Product Marketing) from Pivotal to get their take. Despite rumors that Pivotal’s data business was seeing lackluster success in a busy marketplace, Madra remains bullish, saying that:

    [Pivotal's] data business is thriving

    Which kind of flies in the face of the news this morning that the company is indeed open sourcing all of its big data suite components. Madra justifies this move as a kind of business development decision articulating that:

    We’ve seen tremendous success with open sourcing Cloud Foundry and believe we can see the same commercial success over Hadoop

    Oh and while Madra didn’t clarify the employee cuts directly, he did, somewhat cryptically, state that the layoffs were more about consolidation and delivering geographic clarity for the development part of the business. Anyway – back to the Hadoop thing. There is a precedent for this move away from a proprietary Hadoop distribution – Intel Intel dropped its commercial Hadoop offering and decided to put its support behind Cloudera a year or two back.

    So it is perhaps no surprise to anyone that Pivotal is making this move. In making this decision, beyond the obvious commercial drivers, Pivotal rationalizes the decision due to the following market observations:

    • SQL is today and will remain for the foreseeable future the most valuable workload on Hadoop
    • While Hadoop continues to mature in the enterprise, standalone MPP (massive parallel processing)SQL will remain important
    • Scale out in-memory processing market is healthy and ripe for the taking
    • Streaming and Machine Learning will continue to gain value as workloads over the next 3 years

    The components to be open sourced are:

    • In-Memory NoSQL Database (GemFire)
    • SQL compliant Enterprise SQL on Hadoop (HAWQ)
    • Massive Parallel Processing (MPP) Analytic DB (Greenplum DB)

    Alongside this news Pivotal is broadening core technologies to further distributions of Hadoop. The company rightly points out that Hadoop is a key technology, but a fragmented one and is thus announcing the Open Data Platform. The ODP is a consortium of Hadoop vendors that aims to deliver consistency across the Hadoop ecosystem. Specific aims:

    • Focused on developing common core to enable Hadoop ecosystem
    • Initial members: GE, Hortonworks, IBM, Pivotal, Verizon, and growing. 10+ at launch
    • Focused on core components of Hadoop
    • Rapidly accelerated certifications, ecosystem development, predictability, and enterprise applicability
    • HDFS, MapReduce, YARN, and Ambari with more to follow
    • Members can build, package, and distribute based on this common core

    Pivotal is doubling down on its own technologies. Its HD3 product is focused on adding value on top of a Hadoop core. Via partnerships with other Hadoop partners it is offering its higher-level services – HAWQ, Spark and In-memory workloads. Pivotal HD is designed for the most common workloads that Pivotal has seen. When looking at this news it is hard to look past an obvious fact – you don’t open source a solution that is killing it in the market. Clearly Pivotal is putting a positive spin on a Hadoop distro that didn’t really cut it. That said, Pivotal has enough going on, and sufficient high-value offerings that this move is of little detriment – they’re still in the game, without having to keep trying to ram a proprietary distro down customers’ throats. This is a view that Paul Miller, noted analyst with Cloud of Data reiterated:

    So, at the end of the day, a response to the market reality that Pivotal’s Hadoop distribution is trailing way behind the big three. I’m a little surprised that they’re not simply dropping their own distribution in favor of Hortonworks Data Platform (HDP), but that may be a step too far at this stage. I’d fully expect them to – probably quite quietly – do so late 2015 or early in 2016. That gives them time to demonstrate that HDP connects effectively to the remaining pieces of the Pivotal offering, which are still valuable to customers. Pivotal’s open sourcing of various other pieces of the puzzle is also welcome, although some of them were already available in open source form of course. It’s also quite easy,with hindsight, to suggest that the enterprise open source trend was staring them in the face all along… and that Pivotal’s *initial* offering should have been open source through and through. There’s some merit in that. Now, though, they need to double down and deliver value with a services-based proposition rather than a software one. There’s a big valuation – and a big GE investment – to continue justifying.

    It will be interesting to watch Pivotal maneuvering with regards to its big data intentions over the next few months.
