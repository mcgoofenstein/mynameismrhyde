    Cloud Foundry Foundation Board Appoints Sam Ramji As CEO

    Comment Now
    Follow Comments Following Comments Unfollow Comments

    Ever since the Cloud Foundry Platform as a Service (PaaS) was launched, commentators have been impressed with its growth. In the case of an open source project however, with growth comes questions about governance. In the case of Cloud Foundry this is doubly so given that the project was first launched within VMware and is now part of Pivotal. Clearly other members of the Cloud Foundry ecosystem feel some concerns about the tension between Pivotal’s commercial drivers and those of the broader open source project.

    It is for this reason that the Cloud Foundry Foundation was formed. The Cloud Foundry Foundation is a not-for-profit 501(c)6 independent open source organization established to drive long-term growth and health of Cloud Foundry. Itself sitting within the Linux Foundation, the Cloud Foundry Foundation board is a who’s who of the Cloud Foundry world and includes:

    • ActiveState – Bart Copeland
    • EMC – John Roese
    • HP – Bill Hilf
    • IBM – Christopher Ferris
    • Intel Intel – Nicholas Weaver
    • Pivotal – Rob Mee
    • SAP – Sanjay Patil
    • Swisscom Swisscom – Marco Hochstrasser
    • VMware VMware – Ajay Patel

    The foundation is going a step further today with the announcement that Sam Ramji, highly respected tech industry executive and open source aficionado, has been appointed as CEO of the foundation. The important thing with Ramji is that he has no commercial ties with any of the companies that are members of the foundation – this is critical as it fosters a level playing field within the foundation.

    Most recently Ramji led up strategy for API management vendor Apigee – however there is no untold secret in his leaving – with his resignation he is stepping up and joining the Apigee advisory board. Prior to Apigee, Ramji helped design and lead Microsoft’s open source business and helmed product strategy for BEA WebLogic Integration.

    It’s good timing for Cloud Foundry. The initiative has seen some real momentum including customer wins with two of the top three U.S. telcos and seven Global 500 manufacturing companies using Cloud Foundry. Appointing someone of Ramji’s stature is a coup for the initiative at large.
