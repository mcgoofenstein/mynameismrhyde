Cisco Gets Six Price Target Hikes After Q2 Earnings

Thu, Feb 12 2015 00:00:00 E 00_WEB

BY PETE BARLAS, INVESTOR'S BUSINESS DAILY

11:22 AM ET
  • Email
  • Print
  • Reprints
  • Comment

Cisco Systems (NASDAQ:CSCO) appears to be back on track after the No. 1 computer networking equipment maker late Wednesday reported what it called its best across-the-board results in three years, beating estimates.

The company's outlook for the current quarter was mostly in line.

Analysts cheered. At least six investment banks raised their price targets on Cisco stock, which was up nearly 8% in morning trading in the stock market today, near 29, its highest point since December 2007.

 View Enlarged Image

Wunderlich Securities bumped its price target to 29 from 28 while maintaining its hold rating. Oppenheimer increased its price target to 30 from 29 and kept its outperform rating. RBC Capital Markets hiked its price target to 31 from 29, and also kept its outperform rating. Sterne, Agee & Leach upped its price target to 31 a share from 28. It has a buy rating on the company. FBN Securities increased its price target to 35 from 30. It has an outperform rating on the company. S&P Capital IQ increased its rating to a buy from a hold and hiked its price target to 34 from 27.

Cisco is hitting on almost all cylinders after posting year-over-year revenue growth in nearly every product category, Jason Ader, an analyst for William Blair & Co., said in a research report late Wednesday. He rates Cisco stock outperform.

"Cisco saw the best balance in its business in 12 quarters, with virtually all regions, verticals and product lines showing growth," he wrote. "While Cisco clearly benefitted from easy comparisons across its business and several major product cycles, we nonetheless walked away with increased confidence in the company's business momentum, growth prospects and strategic positioning."

Alex Kurtz, an analyst for Sterne Agee, says improvement in offshore markets are one of the upsides from the quarter.

"The biggest takeaway that should drive incremental support in the stock near term is the improving tone on emerging markets (India plus 11%, Mexico plus 21%) along with U.S. commercial strength up 12% against a relatively tough comp (total Americas up 7% along with EMEA up 7%)," he wrote in a report.

Cisco appears on track to making its transition to becoming an all-purpose IT service company, Kurtz says.

"Services in higher growth segments like cloud, analytics and security all grew double digits as well, suggesting Cisco is slowly becoming a more strategic vendor (relative to traditional data center companies like IBM (NYSE:IBM) and Hewlett-Packard (NYSE:HPQ)," he wrote.

Last month, Cisco attempted to shed light on corporate security concerns with the results of a new survey.
