IBM CEO Ginni Rometty's cloud efforts get major vote of confidence from Warren Buffett

Caroline McMillan Portillo, Bizwomen reporter
Feb 18, 2015, 11:54am EST

Drew Angerer/Bloomberg News

As Ginni Rometty undertakes one of the biggest tasks of her tenure — moving the company to the cloud — the IBM CEO just got a major vote of confidence from one of the most prominent investors in the world.

As Ginni Rometty undertakes one of the biggest tasks of her tenure — moving the company to the cloud — the IBM CEO just got a major vote of confidence from one of the most prominent investors in the world.

Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway Inc. increased its stake in IBM by 9.2 percent, Bloomberg reported, based on a U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission document the company filed Tuesday.

Berkshire Hathaway's stake in the company is now 7.8 percent, and the Omaha, Neb.-based company remains Big Blue's largest stockholder.

Rometty came under fire last month when she received a $3.6 million incentive payout (see the SEC document here), as well as a salary boost of 6.7 percent to $1.6 million — her first since becoming chief executive in 2012. The sticking point: The raise came soon after the company announced its first per-share decline in more than a decade.

But Armonk, N.Y.-based IBM is making headway with its goal to become a contender in the increasing cloud-based tech space. In November, Rometty announced that IBM would be launching a web-based email service designed to rival Gmail and Yahoo Mail.

And in December, Dutch bank ABN Amro signed a 10-year, multibillion-dollar deal for IBM to provide computer infrastructure services for the company's cloud systems.

Rometty, who ranks No. 1 on Fortune's list of the most powerful women in business, says cloud-based systems are the future of the industry.

"They are truly going to change the profile of this company," Rometty said in a May interview with CNBC."And if you think about it, they're going to change the profile of the industry. …The industry is re-ordering."
