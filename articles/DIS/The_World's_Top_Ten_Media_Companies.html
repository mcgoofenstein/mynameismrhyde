The World's Top Ten Media Companies

By Shobhit Seth | February 18, 2015 AAA | 

Media covers a wide variety of streams – advertising, broadcasting and networking, news, print and publication, digital, recording and motion pictures (with associated infrastructure for each). Media companies operate within these streams and offer products and services to end users from individuals to large organizations. Here are the top 10 global media companies with a peek into their businesses and operations. The list is provided in the decreasing order of market cap figures, as available in early 2015.

  • Walt Disney (DIS): A large media and entertainment group with multiple subsidiaries and an international presence, Walt Disney was founded in 1923 and is headquartered in Burbank, California. Its five segments cover the following:
  1. Media Networks (everything within TV, radio, and cable networks, and related operations)
  2. Parks and Resorts (theme parts, hotels, resorts, sports complex, dining and entertainment facilities, and water sports establishments)
  3. Studio Entertainment (live action and animated pictures for distribution in US through subsidiaries and worldwide through partner companies)
  4. Consumer Products (licensing and retailing of trade names, characters and properties, plus educational books and magazines)
  5. Interactive Media (produces online and mobile games, and gaming consoles)

It has a market cap of $173.5 billion and revenues of $48.813 billion as of December 31, 2014.

  • Comcast Corp (CMCSA): Established in 1963 with headquarters in Philadelphia, Comcast is one of the largest global media, entertainment and communications companies listed on the NASDAQ. Its business is run through five segments:
  1. Cable Communications (video, high-speed Internet, and voice services)
  2. Cable Networks (national, regional, sports, news, international cable television networks)
  3. Broadcast Television (Telemundo and NBC)
  4. Filmed Entertainment (films and plays)
  5. Theme Parks (in Orlando and Hollywood)

It has a market cap of $148 billion and trailing twelve-month revenues of $67.97 billion as of September 30, 2014.

  • Twenty-First Century Fox Inc. (FOX): Twenty-First Century Fox is focused on television broadcasting and film production. Its business operations are split across four major segments:
  1. Cable Network Programming (production, licensing, program distribution, cable and satellite broadcast in Asia, Europe, Latin America, and the US)
  2. Filmed Entertainment (production of movies for global licensing and distribution)
  3. Television (broadcasting and operations of network programming and TV stations)
  4. Direct Broadcast Satellite Television (broadcast services in Italy, Germany, and Austria)

It has a market cap of $72.44 billion and annual revenues of $31.86 billion as of December 31, 2014.

  • Time Warner Inc. (TWX): Headquartered in New York, Time Warner is a global media entertainment business. It has three segments of operations: 
  1. Networks (Turner broadcasting and HBO that include local and international brands like TNT, TBS, CNN, and Cartoon Network; pay TV; and associated websites like CNN.com.)
  2. Filmed Entertainment (motion pictures, TV shows, and videogames under the Warner Bros. brand)
  3. Publishing (books and magazine publications, related websites)

It has a market cap of $67.64 billion and annual revenues for 2014 were $7.53 billion.

  • DirecTV (DTV): Based in El Segundo and operational across the US and Latin America, DirecTV is a digital TV entertainment service provider via a satellite network. Split into three segments namely, DIRECTV US, DIRECTV Latin America and DIRECTV Sports Networks, the firm has a market cap of $44.16 billion and revenues of $32.9 billion as of September 2014.
  • Time Warner Cable (TWC): Time Warner Cable Inc. has a wide portfolio of products and service. It provides video, high-speed data, and voice services in the US through its own broadband cable systems. Additionally, it also offers networking, advertising and transport services. NaviSite, a subsidiary company, offers IT solutions and cloud services. Time Warner Cable was established in the year 2003 with headquarters in New York. The parent company has a market cap of $40.9 billion and 2014 annual revenue of $22.81 billion.
  • DISH Network Corporation (DISH): The Dish Network operates as a parent holding company. Through its multiple subsidiaries, it provides TV services through direct satellite broadcast, broadband services, movies and games on demand for rental and sale. It was established in 1980 in Englewood, Colorado. It has a market cap of $35.5 billion and revenues of $14.5 billion.
  • WPP Plc. (WPPGY): Established in 1985 and based in London, WPP acts as a parent holding company. Through its subsidiaries, it provides communications services across multiple segments. Popular brands owned by WPP include JWT, Ogilvy & Mather, Y&R and Grey. Its services include marketing communications, reputation management, brand management, and lobbying services. Its market cap is $29.25 billion and trailing twelve months revenues were $16.98 billion for June 2014. WPP is listed on the London Stock Exchange.
  • CBS Corporation (CBS): CBS Corp is in the business of creating and distributing media content to global audience across multiple platforms. It operates across TV, radio, and print, as well as advertising services, including the ownership and management of ad spaces like billboards, transit paths, benches, trains, buses, etc. It was established in 2005 and is headquartered in New York. It has a market cap of $29.60 billion and annual revenues of $14.98 billion as of September 2014.
  • Viacom Inc. (VIAB): Established in 2005 in New York, Viacom is a creator and provider of entertainment content for global audience through digital (online and mobile), film, and television. Popular brands include Nickelodeon, TeenNick, Comedy Central, Spike TV, Centric, etc. It also operates gaming businesses through websites like AddictingGames.com and Shockwave.com. Its filmed entertainment business includes brand names like Paramount Pictures, MTV Films, Nickelodeon, etc. It has a market cap of $27.62 billion and annual revenues of $13.78 billion as of December 2014.

The Bottom Line

Media is a diverse field. Many companies in the “media” category also operate businesses that may not necessarily qualify as media, such as software solutions, lobbying services, etc. Investors looking for investments in specifically media companies should carefully study the different business segments, operational areas, business domains, and corporate structure to ensure the desired companies fit into their investment profile.
