    Hollywood's Mega Summer Puts These Media Stocks in Focus - Analyst Blog

    By Zacks Equity Research February 17, 2015 2:33 PM
    • 
    • 
    • 
    • 
    Content preferences

      Summer 2015 is already hyped to be one of the biggest movie seasons ever for Hollywood. The lineup of big budget releases, mostly popular franchise players, from almost every studio is what both movie buffs and distributors are looking forward to.

      With movies like Avengers: Age of Ultron, Jurassic World, Terminator: Genisys, Minions, Mission Impossible 5 and The Fantastic Four, lined up for release, this summer easily has the potential to set the box office on fire.

      Will the Season Live Up to the Hype?

      Analysts observe that the sequel/reboot/adaptation model has worked well for Hollywood over the last few years. Last year, almost every highest grossing movie was either an installment of a popular franchise (Transformers: Age of Extinction, X-Men: Days of Future Past, The Hunger Games 3 or Captain America 2, Amazing Spider Man 2, The Dawn of Planet of Apes) or book adaptations (Guardians of the Galaxy and Maleficent). The only exception was Christopher Nolan’s Interstellar, an ‘original' movie which has received world wide acclaim. Transformers 4 emerged #1 with over $1 billion in worldwide collections as per Box Office Mojo.

      In 2013, box office trends were pretty much the same with adaptations/ installments like Iron Man 3, Despicable Me 2, Hobbit 2, Hunger Games 2, Fast and Furious 6, Thor 2 and Superman reboot, Man of Steel, dominating the scene. 2012 and 2011 fared similarly.

      As a result, trade analysts feel that the strategy has proven to be a winner since the viewers like to come back for familiar stories. Though not 100% risk free, it is still a rewarding formula. Based on past performances, it is safe to assume that the summer of 2015 will be a winner. Moreover, with ever expanding new markets (Asia, especially China), Hollywood’s cataclysmic sagas, topped with extremely powerful visual effects, are likely to stay.

      However, a section of the audience has taken to social media to express concerns over such a ‘watershed’ year. So closely dated, movies run the risk of cannibalizing each others’ business. Some franchises might lose out to others given relative interests, while soaring ticket prices will add to the woes. Moreover, if an overdose of sequels, adaptations and so on back fires, it will leave the studios in a bloodbath as they have put billions of dollars at stake for producing these, jeopardizing their future strategies.

      These views are valid to some extent as the jam packed release schedule for the year has forced a massive change in dates already. Some mega productions like Batman Vs Superman, Independence Day 2, and Pirates of the Caribbean 5 have been reportedly moved to 2016. More reshuffling might be in the offing.

      Nevertheless, prima facie, everything looks good for the movie business in 2015.

      Stocks in Focus

      We bring you key media stocks that are most likely to take center stage as Hollywood kicks of its official summer with Avengers: Age of Ultron on May 1.

      The Walt Disney Company (DIS):  Obviously, this one is a no brainer. With Avengers 2 Disney is looking at another great year. The superheroes team re-uniting to fight Ultron is what movie buffs have been waiting for three years now. The Avengers, released in 2012, not only had a fantastic opening but with a box office tally of over $1.5 billion, it is the third highest grossing film of all time behind Avatar and Titanic.

      No wonder the sequel is already being labeled as a billion dollar movie. Apart from that, Disney has Ant-Man, another Marvel superhero film, on Jul 17. Given the buzz surrounding Marvel studios these days and runaway success of its movies, Ant-Man is likely to deliver.

      Also, Pixar will be releasing animated fantasy comedy, Inside Out on Jun 19 that deals with the emotions of an 11 year old girl. Pixar known for its family movies has always proved a winner for Disney. Inside Out is Pixar’s offering after a long gap. No wonder kids and parents alike will be flocking to the theatres.

      Tommorowland, a sci-fi adventure from Disney, is slated for May 22 release. Directed by Oscar winner Brad Bird and starring George Clooney, the movie is generating a lot of interest among the niche audience.

      Given an impressive slate, this Zacks Rank #2 (Buy) stock is set to continue its dream run. The success of Frozen, followed by Captain America, Maleficent and Guardians of the Galaxy, the company has outperformed the Zacks Consensus Estimate in the trailing four quarters by an average of 10.6%. Further the long-term EPS growth rate of this Burbank-based company stands at 9.6%.

      Comcast Corporation (CMCSA): Dinosaurs once again will be unleashing terror, on screens, of course, courtesy Universal Pictures, for reviving the much loved Jurassic Park franchise. Jurassic World, which hits screens on Jun 12, is made with an estimated budget of $150 million and the fourth movie of the franchise. Earlier movies have been monstrous hits, particularly Jurassic Park, released in 1993, with over $1 billion in worldwide collections. Add to it, Chris Pratt, and the movie is undoubtedly a widely anticipated one.

      Going ahead, Universal pictures will release Ted 2 on Jun 26. Ted 2 is a sequel to Ted, released in 2012. Ted is the one of the highest grossing R rated comedy movies and hence expectations from its sequel are quite high.

      Minions, spin off of the hugely popular Despicable Me franchise, is another offering from Universal that is likely to smash box office records when it releases on Jul 10. Despicable Me 2 released in 2013 earned over approximately $970 million at the box office.

      Comcast Film division has already started the year on a high note. Fifty Shades of Grey, released last weekend has already set cash registers jingling. With Furious 7 slated for an April release, the company has set a positive tone for the year.

      Philadelphia-based Comcast, a Zacks Rank #3 (Hold) stock, has outperformed the Zacks Consensus Estimate in the trailing four quarters by an average of 2.6%. Long-term EPS growth rate of the company is 11.9%.

      Paramount Pictures, a unit of Viacom, Inc. (VIAB) is not far behind. Arnold Schwarzenegger is back as the Terminator in Terminator: Genisys (July 1) and so is Tom Cruise as IMF agent Ethan Hunt in Mission Impossible 5 (Jul 31). Both the franchises have earlier proven to be money minting for the studio.

      Twenty-First Century Fox, Inc. (FOXA) will be rebooting Marvel’s first family, The Fantastic Four, scheduled for release on Aug 7. Apart from this, Melissa Mc McCarthy starrer Spy (May 22) and horror flick Poltergeist (Jul 24) both distributed by Fox Studio are relatively small films with high expectations.

      We also have Mad Max: Fury Road (May 15) and Pan (Jul 24) from Warner Brothers, a division of Time Warner Inc. (TIME).

      Not only the season (May to August), but the rest of the year is equally exciting in terms of big budget releases. After the releases of Taken 3, Sponge Bob: Sponge Out Of Water, Fifty Shades of Grey, Cinderella (live action), Insurgent and Furious 7, in the initial part of the year and a choc a bloc summer, rest of the year would have the likes of Lions Gate Entertainment Corp’s (LGF) The Hunger Games: Mockingjay Part 2, Maze Runner: The Scorch Trials, The Good Dinosaur, Monster Trucks, Crimson Peak, Alvin and the Chipmunks: Road Chip , Victor Frankenstein, Paranormal Activity: Ghost Dimension and Spectre (James Bond flick)

      Lastly, release of Star Wars: The Force Awakens on Dec 15 will be a befitting end to a spectacular year.

      Let’s keep fingers crossed.
